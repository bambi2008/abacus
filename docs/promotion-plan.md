# Promotion Plan

## Primary channel: content around the Mint shutdown + gamification angle

Unlike HeelEase/Regimen, this category has a live, ongoing news hook: Mint's
2024 shutdown left millions of users actively searching for alternatives,
and "best Mint alternatives 2026" content is a proven, evergreen search
category (visible in this session's own research — dozens of publishers
have written this exact roundup). This is a real SEO/content opportunity
that didn't exist for the previous two projects: **content explicitly
targeting "Mint alternative" search intent**, positioned specifically
against the automation-first incumbents (Monarch, Copilot) on the "we don't
touch your bank credentials" and "budgeting that doesn't feel like
homework" angles.

## ASO keyword strategy

Target: "budget app no bank account," "manual expense tracker," "budgeting
app without bank sync," "gamified budget app," "envelope budgeting app" —
these map to real, demonstrated search intent (Goodbudget/Finny already
rank on privacy-first budgeting terms) without competing head-on for
"budget app" generically, where Monarch/YNAB/Copilot dominate with
established brand recognition.

## Content angle

Short-form content demonstrating the actual gamified loop (streak saved,
buddy-streak accountability, the loss-aversion notification moment) —
this is a more demonstrable, visual hook than HeelEase's slow-moving
health metrics or Regimen's sensitive photo-analysis flow. "I almost lost
my 47-day budgeting streak" is an inherently more shareable content format
than either previous project's core loop.

## What's copyable vs. not

**Copyable**: YNAB's own community-building playbook — its 250K-member
Reddit community wasn't paid acquisition, it was built through genuinely
useful methodology content (YNAB's "four rules" are widely taught/shared
organically). A similar content-education strategy (teaching the gamified-
habit approach to budgeting, not just marketing the app) is a proven path
in this exact category.

**Not copyable**: Monarch's post-Mint-shutdown 20x growth was a one-time
market disruption event, not a repeatable acquisition channel — don't plan
around another incumbent collapsing.

## Realistic assessment

This category's SEO/content space is more mature and competitive than
HeelEase's or Regimen's (more publishers, more established sites already
ranking on budgeting-app comparison content) — organic distribution will
likely be slower to break into here than it was in the newer, less-
saturated categories explored earlier this session, even though the
underlying demand and willingness-to-pay are better proven.
